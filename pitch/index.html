reading the graph

Two moments change everything.

For the first stretch, cost and value climb together, cost slightly ahead, you are paying to get the team up to speed. Then two things happen.

the first marker · upload

TOP-1 starts forecasting

Nothing leaves your side. TOP-1 reads your usage privately and anonymised, inside your own environment, never your code or your prompts, only aggregate numbers. From that it prices every task before it runs, as a calibrated band with a hard P90 ceiling. Nothing bends yet, it measures and caps, and you stop being surprised by the bill.

the second marker · implementation

TOP-2 bends the curve

The optimiser switches on and routes each task to the cheapest model that still meets the bar. The cost line bends down while output value keeps climbing. The green wedge between the lines is the surplus, more value for less spend.

the honest twist

The Jevons effect.

In 1865 the economist William Stanley Jevons noticed something strange about coal. A better steam engine used less coal per unit of work, so everyone expected total coal use to fall. It rose. Cheaper steam power made it worth using in far more places, and demand outran the saving.

AI is the same shape. Intelligence is cheap now and getting cheaper, and the appetite for it is close to bottomless. Make each task cheaper and a team does not spend less, it does more. Microsoft's Satya Nadella said exactly this when cheaper models arrived: efficiency will not shrink AI spend, it will expand what AI is used for.

So a promise to "cut your AI bill in half" rarely survives contact with reality. Make each task cheaper and your teams simply do more with the headroom, and the saving turns into more work rather than a smaller invoice. That is the right-hand side of the slider: hold your spend steady and your output climbs far faster.

the risk

Chase a smaller bill on its own and Jevons quietly takes it back. Usage grows to fill the space the saving opened up, and the number never quite lands on your invoice.

the opportunity

Aim for far more output from the same budget and Jevons works for you. And because your AI use will only grow, you need a way to forecast and govern it, which is exactly what TOP-1 and TOP-2 give you. The rebound makes that control layer more valuable, not less.

So the number that matters is neither the cost nor the value on its own. It is the ratio: how much value each euro of tokens buys. Whether you bank the saving or reinvest it, TOP moves that ratio in your favour. The slider is your call, take the saving or take the growth, and either way you are on a better curve than the one you are on today.
