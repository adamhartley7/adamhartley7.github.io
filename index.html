the problem

AI spend is invisible until the bill lands.

You pay per token, but the cost of any given task is unknowable until after it runs. Three failures follow.

📉

You can't budget or cap

No sane per-request ceiling exists when the bill is invisible until the work is already done.

🔀

Routing inverts

Trivial work gets sent to a frontier model out of habit; hard reasoning gets pushed to a cheap one to "save money" — degrading exactly what needed the strong model.

🔥

Spend runs away

An agent loop with no cost foresight can burn a budget with nothing standing in the way.

how it works

A calibrated forecast, per request.

Everything else measures spend after it happens. TOP prices it before you commit.

01

Classify the task

An ensemble sorts the request into an archetype — code, refactor, research, chat — each carrying a cost distribution learned from real logged history. Never an invented number.

02

Blend with your usage

It blends that shared prior with your own logged actuals (empirical-Bayes, in log-space) — sensible on day one, sharper the more you run.

03

Forecast & guard

You get a P10 / P50 / P90 band. A spend-guard takes the P90 and enforces a hard ceiling — the design fails closed rather than advising and hoping.

privacy

We never see your code or your prompts.

Data handling is the centre of the arrangement — not an afterthought.

✓
Local parse. Your session logs are featurised on your machine. Only anonymised, aggregate numbers ever leave your environment.
✓
Aggregates only. Counts, distributions, log-token statistics and cost totals — never the underlying prompts or completions.
✓
Inspectable. The parsing script contains no network code. Read it, search it for fetch(, run it yourself.
✓
Consent & NDA. Opt-in, revocable, with an auditable manifest of exactly which fields leave the machine — all under a signed NDA.
