why it matters

AI costs are hard to control before the work starts.

A team usually sees the price only after a task is finished. That creates three practical problems.

01

No budget before the task

You cannot set a sensible limit when you do not know whether a task is likely to cost cents or euros.

02

The wrong tool gets used

Teams often use an expensive model for simple work, or a cheaper model for work that needs stronger reasoning.

03

Automated work keeps spending

Long-running AI jobs can continue using money without a clear warning or stopping point.

how it works

Estimate the cost before you press run.

TOP-1 is being tested on historical usage to see whether it can estimate a pre-run cost range. Forecasting is not yet available in the public analyzer.

01

Understand the job

TOP groups the request by the kind of work, such as coding, research, or a quick answer.

02

Learn from real usage

It compares that kind of task with past jobs, including your own history when available.

03

Show a useful range

It shows a lower, likely, and higher cost. A future control could stop work above your chosen limit, but that control is not live yet.

Planned
An automatic limit that can stop overspending

Early evidence is limited. In one internal chronological backtest on a single founder's Claude Code history, 773 of 849 sessions (91.0%) were one repeated workflow. In the 63 held-out sessions outside that workflow, the description-only median forecast was about 3.3 times the actual API-equivalent cost. This is an internal single-user result, not a general accuracy claim.
